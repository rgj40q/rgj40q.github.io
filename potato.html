This page is about the conlang 'Prominent Potato' (PP).

I. Phonology and orthography

Letters include a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, i, j, k, l, m, n, o, p, r, s, t, u, v, x, z. Capital letters are not used.

Letters are pronounced as in IPA except: c /ʃ/, j /ʒ/, and h /ɣ/. NB: x /x/.

Consonants include m, n, r, l, b, d, g, p, t, k, v, z, j, h, f, s, c, x.

Vowels include:

  • monophthongs i, u, e, o, a;
  • rising diphthongs iu, ie, ue, io, uo, ia, ua;
  • falling diphthongs ui, ei, eu, oi, ou, ai, au.

Syllable structure is CV in stem, and CV(C) in ending.

Borrowed words and personal names that do not fit in these rules must be adapted with either throwing away offending sounds, or inserting additional sounds to break up clusters. The default choice is 'h /ɣ/' for hiatus and 'e' for consonant clusters.

PP is a syllable-timed language. The weak accent is on the first syllable. There is little to no vowel reduction. Intonation is used for emphasis.
